Days 2 & 3: Final Clearing

Completing vegetation removal and debris clearing.

Days 2 & 3 Progress

After nearly two months of rain delays, we were finally able to resume the project. A small group of Scouts joined me to tackle the lot once again, which had become overgrown after weeks of wet weather. We focused on removing the new weeds, loosening the compacted soil, and beginning preparations for grading and irrigation layout. With the help of both Scouts and adult volunteers, we made significant progress in restoring the site to a workable condition.

Accomplishments

  • Removed most of the weeds that had regrown during the rain delay.
  • Tilled and graded the lot to create a smooth, even surface for future landscaping.
  • Unraveled and cleared the maze of old, broken sprinkler lines scattered throughout the property.
  • Improved the overall layout and organization of the worksite for upcoming installation days.

Teamwork

The success of these workdays came from strong cooperation between Scouts, leaders, and adult volunteers. Despite the smaller turnout, everyone contributed steadily throughout the weekend, working side by side to dig, till, and clean up the area. The shared effort demonstrated how consistent teamwork and communication could overcome delays and physical challenges, setting the foundation for the next stages of the project.
