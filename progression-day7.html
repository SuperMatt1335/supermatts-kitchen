Day 7: Finalizing and Compacting the Pathways

Completing and reinforcing the decomposed granite pathways to ensure durability and a professional finish.

Day 7 Progress

With most of the decomposed granite already in place from the previous workdays, our focus today was on strengthening and sealing the pathways. I was able to secure an organic binding agent to mix into the DG, which would help solidify the surface and prevent future erosion. We also rented a heavy-duty compactor to achieve a dense, stable finish. By the end of the day, the pathways were fully compacted and ready to handle foot traffic, marking a major milestone toward completing the landscaping phase.

Accomplishments

  • Applied organic binding agent to the decomposed granite for long-term durability.
  • Rented and operated a mechanical compactor to fully compress the pathways.
  • Completed final grading and smoothing, ensuring the walkways were level and consistent throughout the garden.
  • Prepared the site for upcoming installations, including benches, statues, and new plantings.

Technical Learning

This workday provided valuable hands-on experience with materials and equipment typically used in professional landscaping. I learned how to properly mix and apply a binding agent to stabilize decomposed granite and how to operate a plate compactor safely and effectively. Understanding the importance of moisture control, even distribution, and timing during compaction was key to achieving a smooth, durable finish. These lessons emphasized the balance between preparation, precision, and technique in creating lasting outdoor features.
